Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman

The Brilliance that Redefined our Understanding of Light

Here's a time line of Dr. CV Raman's life:

  • 1888 :

    November 7,Born in Tiruchirapalli, Tamil Nadu, India.

  • 1902 :

    Enrolled at the Presidency College in Chennai (then Madras) to pursue a bachelor's degree in science.

  • 1904 :

    Completed his Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree with honors in physics and continued his studies at the Presidency College.

  • 1907 :

    Received his Master of Sciences (MSc) degree in physics from the University of Madras.

  • 1907-1917 :

    Appointed as an Assistant Accountant General in the Indian Finance Department but continued his scientific research alongside his job.

  • 1921 :

    Appointed as the first Palit Professor of Physics at the University of Calcutta.

  • 1928 :

    Discovered the Raman Effect, the scattering of light that revealed the change in wavelength due to the interaction of light with molecules, for which he would later receive the Nobel Prize.

  • 1929 :

    Published his findings on the Raman Effect in the journal "Nature".

  • 1930 :

    Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the scattering of light and the discovery of the Raman Effect.

  • 1933 :

    Knighted as "Sir" by the British government, becoming Sir C.V. Raman.

  • 1943 :

    Established the Raman Research Institute (RRI) in Bangalore, India, dedicated to research in physics.

  • 1947 :

    Became the Director of the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) in Bangalore.

  • 1954 :

    Received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.

  • 1970 :

    Passed away on November 21 in Bangalore, India, at the age of 82.
